## Set Up Your First Publication (The 15-Minute Version)

Getting started on Beehiiv is genuinely fast. You can be publishing within minutes of signing up, with no credit card required on the free plan. The onboarding flow asks about your newsletter topic and helps you configure a basic website — Beehiiv treats your newsletter as a publication, not just an email blast.

Here's the essential first-session checklist:

1. **Create your account and publication** — choose your newsletter name, URL slug, and basic branding.
2. **Authenticate your sending domain** — domain authentication via SPF, DKIM, and DMARC is essential for deliverability and reputation in 2026, helping you avoid spam flags and improve inbox placement. Beehiiv makes this step relatively painless, with clear guidance — it's one of the most important five minutes you'll spend in setup.
3. **Set up your website** — the free Launch plan includes a website builder that lets you create a landing page to capture signups and showcase published articles, with the option to connect a custom domain.
4. **Draft and send a test email** — Beehiiv's editor is clean and distraction-free. It's built more like a blog CMS than a typical email builder — no conventional drag-and-drop editor, but it makes publishing fast.

**Pro tip:** Beehiiv uses a pre-authenticated beehiiv.com domain until your own domain has been warmed up, so deliverability is protected from day one.

## Understanding Beehiiv's Pricing Tiers

Most guides gloss over the pricing gap, but it's the single biggest friction point creators face. Beehiiv's Launch plan is free, forever, with no credit card required — you get up to 2,500 subscribers, unlimited email sends, and core newsletter, website, and podcast tools. Paid plans start at $49/month.

Here's the honest breakdown:

- **Launch (Free):** Up to 2,500 subscribers, unlimited sends, custom website, basic analytics, recommendation network, 3 publications. You don't get monetization (no Ad Network, Boosts, paid subscriptions, or digital products), no A/B testing, no AI features, no automations, and no referral program.
- **Scale ($49/mo):** Unlocks the ad network, Boosts, paid subscriptions, automations, and human support.
- **Max ($109/mo):** Adds white-labeling (removing Beehiiv branding), audio newsletters, a sponsorship storefront, up to 10 publications, and unlimited team seats.

The real concern? The jump from $0 to $49 is the sticking point — there's no intermediate tier for creators who've outgrown 2,500 free subscribers but aren't yet ready to monetize seriously. ## Monetize From Day One: Ads, Boosts, and Paid Subscriptions

This is where Beehiiv genuinely separates from the pack. The 0% take rate on paid subscriptions is the headline differentiator — Substack takes 10% of every subscription, while Beehiiv takes nothing beyond Stripe's standard 2.9% + $0.30 fee. For a newsletter with 500 paying subscribers at $10/month, that's $600/month more in your pocket.

### The Ad Network

The Beehiiv Ad Network is a standout feature — instead of hunting down sponsors yourself, Beehiiv connects you with advertisers who want to reach newsletter audiences, and ads are automatically matched to your newsletter. Many creators report earning $200–$500+ per issue once they hit 5,000+ engaged subscribers.

### Boosts

Boosts is Beehiiv's unique cross-promotion system that lets you earn money by recommending other newsletters to your audience — and grow your own list by paying to be recommended by others. According to Beehiiv's Boosts page, the average cost of one active subscriber is $1.63.

### Paid Subscriptions and Paywalls

You can write "above the fold" content (what free subscribers see) and "below the fold" content (paywalled for paid subscribers) in the same editor. The recently launched customizable paywalls let you A/B test messaging, pricing tiers, and perks to optimize conversions.

## What Most Guides Miss: Real Limitations to Know

No platform is perfect, and the glossy reviews often skip the rough edges that matter most in daily use.

**Automation is limited.** Triggers are fairly basic, and complex behavioral sequences are harder to build here than in something like ActiveCampaign or Klaviyo. Some users report that email flows and automations are limited to 30-day timeframes, making it difficult to import longer sequences.

**Third-party integrations lean on Zapier.** Integrations rely heavily on Zapier rather than native connectors, which adds friction and cost for anyone running a complex tech stack.

**Support has gaps.** The absence of live chat or phone support across all paid tiers is a limitation if you run a time-sensitive publication. Beehiiv reserves contact with the Support team for paid plan users or users in their first 30 days.

**Design flexibility is minimal.** Some customization options feel limited, especially when trying to fine-tune design and overall layout. If you need pixel-perfect branded emails, Flodesk or Mailchimp's block editor may serve you better.
