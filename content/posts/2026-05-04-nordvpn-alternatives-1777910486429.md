## The Top NordVPN Alternatives for 2026

### 1. ExpressVPN — Best for Raw Speed and Streaming

ExpressVPN has consistently posted the highest speed benchmarks among mainstream VPNs, with independent tests showing throughput exceeding 10Gbps on their Lightway protocol. In my testing, it was the most reliable option for accessing geo-restricted streaming libraries across Netflix, Disney+, BBC iPlayer, and Amazon Prime Video.

**What stands out:**
- Lightway protocol delivers exceptional performance with minimal overhead
- RAM-only server infrastructure (TrustedServer) — no data survives a reboot
- Based in the British Virgin Islands, outside Five Eyes jurisdiction
- Consistent unblocking success across streaming platforms
- Split tunneling available on most platforms

**Where it falls short:**
- Premium pricing — typically the most expensive option in the market
- Fewer simultaneous connections than some competitors (8 devices)
- Owned by Kape Technologies, which some privacy advocates view with skepticism

### 2. Surfshark — Best Budget Alternative

Surfshark has positioned itself as the value play in the premium VPN market, and it delivers surprisingly well. Unlimited simultaneous connections make it ideal for families or anyone running multiple devices.

**What stands out:**
- Unlimited device connections on a single subscription
- CleanWeb feature blocks ads, trackers, and malware
- MultiHop (double VPN) routing for enhanced privacy
- Solid streaming unblocking — reliably works with Netflix US, UK, and several other libraries
- RAM-only servers across the entire network

**Where it falls short:**
- Speeds are good but not class-leading; noticeable drops on distant servers
- Based in the Netherlands — an EU jurisdiction, which some privacy purists dislike
- Merged with NordVPN's parent company (Nord Security), which may concern users seeking a truly independent alternative

### 3. Mullvad VPN — Best for Privacy Purists

If your primary concern is anonymity rather than streaming convenience, Mullvad is in a league of its own. This Swedish provider doesn't even ask for your email address when you sign up.

**What stands out:**
- Account numbers instead of usernames/emails — genuinely anonymous signup
- Accepts cash payments mailed in an envelope (yes, really)
- Open-source clients on all platforms
- WireGuard implementation is among the cleanest available
- Transparent, flat pricing — no confusing tier structures
- Passed multiple independent audits

**Where it falls short:**
- Limited server network compared to larger providers
- Poor choice for streaming — doesn't prioritize or reliably unblock geo-restricted content
- No dedicated apps for smart TVs or routers
- Flat monthly pricing means no long-term discount savings

### 4. Proton VPN — Best Free Tier and Swiss Privacy

Proton VPN comes from the team behind ProtonMail, and their commitment to privacy is well-documented. They offer the most functional free VPN tier available — no data caps, no ads, no selling your browsing data.

**What stands out:**
- Headquartered in Switzerland with strong legal privacy protections
- Free tier includes unlimited data (rare and legitimate)
- Secure Core architecture routes traffic through privacy-friendly countries
- NetShield ad and malware blocker
- Strong track record of transparency reports
- Full suite integration with ProtonMail, Proton Drive, and Proton Calendar

**Where it falls short:**
- Free tier limited to servers in three countries with reduced speeds
- Paid plans are competitively priced but not the cheapest
- Streaming unblocking on free tier is nonexistent; paid tier performs well but inconsistently on some platforms
- Fewer server locations than ExpressVPN or NordVPN

## The Privacy Jurisdiction Question

One of the most underappreciated factors in choosing a VPN is where the company is legally headquartered. This determines what laws govern data retention and government requests.

Here's the reality: Panama (NordVPN), the British Virgin Islands (ExpressVPN), and Switzerland (Proton VPN) all offer strong privacy protections with no mandatory data retention laws. Sweden (Mullvad) has some data retention requirements, but Mullvad's architecture makes compliance essentially impossible since they don't collect identifiable data in the first place.

The Netherlands (Surfshark) sits within EU jurisdiction, which means GDPR applies — mostly a positive for privacy — but also means the provider is subject to Dutch and EU law enforcement requests.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is ExpressVPN faster than NordVPN?**
In independent benchmarks, ExpressVPN's Lightway protocol consistently edges out NordVPN's NordLynx on raw throughput. The difference is measurable but unlikely to be noticeable for most daily activities. Both are fast enough for 4K streaming and large file downloads.

**Which VPN alternative has the best privacy?**
Mullvad, followed closely by Proton VPN. Mullvad's anonymous signup process and minimal data collection set the standard. Proton VPN benefits from Swiss privacy law and an established reputation through ProtonMail.

**Can NordVPN alternatives unblock streaming services?**
Yes. ExpressVPN is arguably the most reliable for unblocking Netflix, BBC iPlayer, and other geo-restricted platforms. Surfshark and Proton VPN (paid) also perform well, though with occasional inconsistencies. Mullvad is not a good choice for streaming.

**What's the cheapest reliable VPN alternative?**
Surfshark typically offers the lowest pricing on long-term plans while still delivering genuine protection and streaming capabilities. Proton VPN's free tier is the best zero-cost option. For the full picture on what different VPN services currently charge, always check their official sites — pricing in this market shifts constantly.

**Do VPN alternatives keep logs?**
All five providers listed here claim no-logs policies, and all have undergone independent audits to verify those claims. The key differentiator is *how* they architect their systems to make logging technically difficult — RAM-only servers (ExpressVPN, Surfshark, NordVPN) being the gold standard since data is wiped on every reboot.
