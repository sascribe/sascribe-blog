## Key Features Compared

### 1. Newsletter Growth Tools

Beehiiv leads here by a wide margin. Its built-in referral program lets subscribers unlock rewards for sharing your newsletter — the same mechanic Morning Brew used to grow to millions of subscribers. Add the Beehiiv Boost network (where newsletters cross-promote each other for a fee) and Recommendations (free cross-promotion), and you've got a growth engine that no other platform matches natively.

Mailchimp has nothing comparable. Kit offers basic referral integrations through third-party tools like SparkLoop (which, ironically, Beehiiv acquired). Substack has recommendations, but they're limited to other Substack publications.

### 2. Monetization

Substack's entire model is built on paid subscriptions, but it takes a 10% cut of your revenue. That adds up fast. Beehiiv offers paid subscriptions with zero platform fees on its Scale plan, plus an ad network that connects you with brand sponsors — even if you only have a few thousand subscribers. Kit offers paid newsletters too, but lacks the ad marketplace. Mailchimp has no native monetization.

### 3. Email Design and Editing

Mailchimp still has the most mature drag-and-drop email builder. If you want pixel-perfect branded emails with complex layouts, Mailchimp gives you the most control. Beehiiv's editor is clean and fast — optimized for newsletter writing rather than design-heavy campaigns. Kit's editor is deliberately minimal. Substack's is basically a text editor.

### 4. Automation and Sequences

Kit wins the automation game. Its visual automation builder is intuitive and powerful — perfect for complex welcome sequences, tagging logic, and drip campaigns. Mailchimp's automation is solid but can feel clunky. Beehiiv has added automation sequences that handle most common use cases (welcome flows, re-engagement, conditional branching), and they're improving rapidly. Substack has almost no automation capabilities.

### 5. Analytics

Beehiiv's analytics dashboard is best-in-class for newsletters. You get 3D open maps, click heatmaps, subscriber acquisition source tracking, and engagement scoring. Mailchimp provides decent analytics but focuses more on e-commerce metrics. Kit's reporting is functional but basic. Substack shows you open rates and that's about it.

### 6. Website and SEO

Every Beehiiv newsletter gets a fully hosted website with SEO optimization built in. Your posts are indexable, fast-loading, and customizable. This is a genuine differentiator — your newsletter content works double duty as organic search content. Substack offers hosted pages too but with less customization. Mailchimp has landing pages but they're not designed for ongoing content. Kit's landing pages are decent but limited.
