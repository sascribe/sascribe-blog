## Synthesia in 2026: What You're Actually Getting

Synthesia has cemented itself as the enterprise-grade default in AI video generation. The platform converts written scripts into presenter-led videos using AI avatars — no cameras, no studios, no actors. That pitch hasn't changed, but the feature set has matured considerably.

As of April 2026, Synthesia offers **240+ stock AI avatars** representing diverse ages, ethnicities, and professional styles. The platform supports **160+ languages** for narration and AI dubbing with lip-sync across 130+ dialects. The built-in editor now includes interactive elements like clickable CTAs, branching scenarios, and embedded quizzes — features that matter enormously for training teams who need more than a passive talking head.

Recent updates include the **AI Playground**, which gives users access to Google Veo 3.1 and Sora 2 directly inside the platform for generating supplementary video assets. The **PowerPoint-to-video workflow** has been overhauled: upload a deck, and Synthesia converts speaker notes into video scripts while preserving your original slide design as editable components. What sets Synthesia apart from most competitors is its enterprise compliance posture. ## Pricing Breakdown: Synthesia vs HeyGen vs Colossyan

Pricing is where most comparison articles get vague. Here are the actual numbers as of Q1 2026, verified across multiple sources:

**Synthesia:**
- **Free (Basic):** $0/month — 10 minutes/month, 9 avatars, watermarked output
- **Starter:** $29/month ($18/month billed annually) — 10 minutes/month, 125+ avatars, 3 personal avatars, watermark removed
- **Creator:** $89/month ($64/month billed annually) — 30 minutes/month, 180+ avatars, 5 personal avatars, multi-avatar scenes, interactive video, API access
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing — unlimited minutes, 240+ avatars, SSO, SCORM export, dedicated onboarding

**HeyGen:**
- **Free:** Limited minutes, 30+ languages
- **Creator:** $29/month ($24/month annually) — individual use, 175+ languages
- **Business:** $149/month + $20/seat — SCORM, interactivity, priority rendering
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing

**Colossyan:**
- Competitive mid-range pricing between HeyGen and Synthesia
- Pro plan includes scenario branching and LMS-ready features
- 70+ languages with in-editor translation

The critical detail most articles skip: **Synthesia's per-minute cost at Starter is roughly $2.90/minute** of finished video. The jump from Starter to Creator costs $60/month more for 20 additional minutes — a steep leap if you only need 12-15 minutes monthly. HeyGen's Creator plan sits at a similar per-minute rate, but its Business tier unlocks collaboration and SCORM features at $149/month, while Synthesia gates SCORM export behind its custom-priced Enterprise plan.

**Hidden costs to budget for:** High-quality Studio Express-1 avatars carry a **$1,000/year add-on** on Synthesia. Features many L&D teams consider essential — SCORM export, 1-click video translation, SSO — are Enterprise-only. This means mid-sized companies often face a steeper real cost than the pricing page suggests.

## Avatar Quality and Realism: The Practical Differences

This is where the decision gets personal, because avatar quality determines whether your audience watches or cringes.

In head-to-head testing reported across multiple independent reviews, **HeyGen's avatars tend to feel more expressive and dynamic** — better micro-expressions, more natural head tilts, and fluid gestures that work well for short-form marketing content under three minutes. **Synthesia's avatars prioritize consistency and polish across longer videos** — a 10-15 minute training module maintains steady quality without the uncanny drift that can plague other platforms.

Synthesia leads specifically in **multilingual lip-sync accuracy**. Independent testing shows it handles French, Japanese, and Arabic phonemes more precisely than competitors. For global organizations producing the same training video in a dozen languages, this consistency matters more than flashy gestures.

Where Synthesia draws criticism — and this is a recurring theme on review platforms — is the **"uncanny valley" problem** for certain use cases. Some users note that avatars can appear "clinical" and lack the nuanced emotional expressiveness needed for deeply persuasive or empathetic content. Pronunciation accuracy can be inconsistent, particularly in languages other than English, sometimes requiring additional editing cycles.

**Colossyan** sits between both platforms on realism. Its avatars present well for structured training content with accurate lip-sync, but don't match HeyGen's expressiveness or Synthesia's enterprise-grade consistency.

**Bottom line:** If your average video is under 3 minutes and aimed at social or marketing channels, HeyGen's natural energy wins. If you're producing 5-15 minute training modules across multiple languages, Synthesia's stability and lip-sync precision matter more. ## The Content Moderation Issue Nobody Else Covers

Here's the angle almost every competitor comparison ignores — and it's the single biggest source of frustration in real user reviews.

Synthesia enforces strict content moderation policies, and users in **healthcare, biotech, financial services, and medical diagnostics** have reported videos being rejected without clear explanation. One verified G2 reviewer described having content flagged as "medical related" despite it being an investor overview containing no diagnoses or treatment guidance. The workaround? Pay for a custom avatar at $1,000/year, with no guarantee the restriction lifts.

The moderation system has also drawn complaints for **inconsistency** — videos approved one day getting flagged after minor edits like swapping an image or correcting pronunciation. Users describe receiving vague automated responses with no practical appeal process.

This matters for your buying decision. If your organization operates in a regulated industry, **confirm your use case is compliant with Synthesia's Acceptable Use Policy before purchasing.** These restrictions aren't prominently disclosed during sign-up. HeyGen and Colossyan have their own content policies, but user reports suggest less friction for educational and informational content in regulated verticals.

For teams in corporate training, HR, marketing, and agencies producing standard business content, this is unlikely to be an issue. But if you're in healthcare communications or financial services, test with a free account first.

## Use Case Matrix: Which Platform Wins Where

Rather than declaring an overall winner, here's how each platform maps to specific workflows:

### Corporate Training & L&D
**Winner: Synthesia** (or Colossyan for quiz-heavy modules)

Synthesia's interactive video features — branching paths, embedded quizzes, and score tracking — combined with its LMS integration make it the strongest all-in-one option. Colossyan's native quiz and assessment tools are arguably deeper, but Synthesia's broader avatar library and language coverage give it the edge for global training programs. The FOCA scripting framework (Focus, Outcome, Content, Action) taught in Synthesia Academy helps teams structure effective training scripts from day one.

### Marketing Explainers & Product Demos
**Winner: HeyGen** for short-form; **Synthesia** for brand-consistent series

HeyGen's expressive avatars and faster turnaround suit punchy marketing clips. But Synthesia's brand kit enforcement, template library (250+ templates), and AI Screen Recorder — which lets you create product walkthroughs with an AI avatar overlay narrating the demo — make it better for structured product content that needs to stay on-brand across dozens of videos.

### Multilingual Internal Communications
**Winner: Synthesia**

The one-click translation workflow and superior multilingual lip-sync accuracy make Synthesia the clear choice when a CEO's weekly update needs to reach teams in English, Spanish, French, and Japanese simultaneously. Voice cloning paired with custom avatars means the message feels personal even at scale.

### Budget-Conscious Small Teams
**Winner: HeyGen** (Creator plan) or **Elai** for simpler workflows

Synthesia's free plan works for proof-of-concept, but the watermark and limited avatars make it impractical for client-facing work. HeyGen's $24/month annual Creator plan offers more flexibility at a lower entry point. For teams that just need basic text-to-video without enterprise complexity, Elai provides a simpler, more affordable path.
