## How We Compared: Methodology and Criteria

Before diving into the results, here's what we evaluated and why.

### Privacy and Security

We looked at encryption protocols, logging policies, jurisdiction, independent audit history, and advanced features like multi-hop connections, RAM-only servers, and kill switches. A VPN that doesn't protect your data isn't worth using at any price.

### Streaming and Unblocking

The ability to unblock streaming libraries — especially Netflix US, BBC iPlayer, Disney+, and Amazon Prime Video — is one of the top reasons people download a VPN. We tested each provider across multiple regions and platforms.

## NordVPN vs. ExpressVPN: The Flagship Rivalry

This is the comparison everyone wants to see. Both are premium-tier providers with massive server networks and long reputations. But in 2026, the gap between them has widened in a few key areas.

### Privacy

NordVPN operates under Panamanian jurisdiction — outside the 5/9/14 Eyes surveillance alliances. It has completed multiple independent audits of its no-logs policy by PricewaterhouseCoopers and Deloitte. Its entire server fleet runs on RAM-only (diskless) infrastructure, meaning data is wiped on every reboot. Features like Double VPN (multi-hop), Onion Over VPN, and Threat Protection Pro (which blocks trackers, malware, and phishing domains at the network level) put it at the top of the privacy stack.

ExpressVPN, now owned by Kape Technologies (which also owns CyberGhost and Private Internet Access), operates out of the British Virgin Islands and has also passed independent audits. Its TrustedServer technology mirrors the RAM-only approach. However, the Kape ownership has raised eyebrows among privacy advocates due to the parent company's history in the adtech space. For users who weigh corporate lineage heavily, this matters.

### Streaming

Both are excellent for unblocking streaming content. NordVPN consistently accesses Netflix US, UK, Japan, and other regional libraries, plus BBC iPlayer, Hulu, Disney+, and Amazon Prime Video. Its SmartPlay feature handles DNS-level unblocking automatically, making it one of the best VPN for Netflix solutions available. ExpressVPN matches this capability closely, with reliable access to similar libraries via its MediaStreamer DNS feature. This round is close to a draw, but NordVPN's integrated Threat Protection layer adds value by blocking ads during streaming sessions.

### Pricing

Here's where NordVPN pulls ahead decisively. NordVPN's 2-year Standard plan starts at around $3.09/month (billed upfront), while ExpressVPN's longest plan typically runs above $6.67/month. NordVPN also allows up to 10 simultaneous connections on its Standard plan, compared to ExpressVPN's 8. Both offer 30-day money-back guarantees.

**Verdict:** NordVPN wins on price, matched on streaming, and holds an edge on privacy transparency.

## NordVPN vs. Surfshark: Budget Challenger Meets Premium

Surfshark has earned its reputation as a budget-friendly VPN with unlimited simultaneous connections. But does cheaper mean better?

### Privacy

Surfshark recently moved its legal jurisdiction to the Netherlands — an EU country and a member of the 9 Eyes alliance. While the company maintains a strict no-logs policy and has passed independent audits, this jurisdictional choice is a step down from NordVPN's Panamanian base for privacy purists. Surfshark does offer multi-hop, a kill switch, and CleanWeb (its ad and tracker blocker), but NordVPN's Threat Protection Pro is more comprehensive, operating even when the VPN tunnel isn't active.

### Streaming

Surfshark is a solid performer for streaming. It reliably unblocks Netflix, Disney+, BBC iPlayer, and most major platforms. However, some users report more frequent server shuffling — needing to try multiple servers before finding one that works — compared to NordVPN's SmartPlay, which routes traffic more seamlessly. If unblocking streaming is your primary use case, NordVPN's consistency gives it the edge.

### Torrenting

Both support P2P traffic on dedicated servers. NordVPN offers specialized P2P servers optimized for torrent traffic, plus SOCKS5 proxy access for users who want to route torrent clients without the full VPN tunnel. Surfshark supports P2P but without a dedicated SOCKS5 proxy option, which limits flexibility for advanced torrent users.

### Pricing

Surfshark's 2-year plan undercuts NordVPN slightly, often coming in below $2.50/month. Its unlimited device connections are a genuine advantage for households. However, NordVPN's 10-device limit is generous enough for most users, and the additional security features (Threat Protection Pro, Dark Web Monitor, dedicated IP options) justify the price difference.

**Verdict:** Surfshark wins on raw price and device limits. NordVPN wins on privacy jurisdiction, streaming reliability, and security depth.

## NordVPN vs. Proton VPN: The Privacy Purist's Dilemma

Proton VPN, from the makers of ProtonMail, positions itself as the most privacy-forward VPN on the market. It's a serious contender.

### Privacy

Proton VPN is based in Switzerland, which has strong privacy laws and is outside the EU and Eyes alliances. It's open source, has passed independent audits, and integrates with the Tor network via Tor over VPN servers. For users whose top priority is privacy above all else, Proton VPN is a legitimate alternative.

NordVPN counters with its Panamanian jurisdiction (also excellent for privacy), RAM-only servers, Double VPN, and Threat Protection Pro. NordVPN's server network is significantly larger — over 6,400 servers in 111 countries compared to Proton VPN's approximately 4,600+ servers in 91 countries. More servers generally mean less congestion and more options for bypassing geo-restrictions.

### Streaming

This is where NordVPN opens a clear gap. Proton VPN's free tier doesn't support streaming at all, and even its paid plans have historically been less reliable for unblocking Netflix and other platforms. NordVPN's SmartPlay technology was purpose-built for this use case, making it one of the best VPN for streaming on the market. If you want to unblock streaming content consistently, NordVPN is the stronger choice.

### Pricing

Proton VPN offers a free tier — rare among reputable VPNs — but it's limited to servers in five countries with no streaming or P2P support. Its paid plans are more expensive than NordVPN's equivalent tiers. Proton VPN Plus starts around $4.99/month on a 2-year plan compared to NordVPN's ~$3.09/month.

**Verdict:** Proton VPN is a top pick for privacy-first users who don't prioritize streaming. NordVPN offers a better all-around package at a lower price. [Try NordVPN](https://sascribe.com/go/nordvpn) if you want the best balance of privacy, streaming access, and value.

## NordVPN vs. CyberGhost: The Ease-of-Use Factor

CyberGhost markets itself as the most user-friendly VPN, with servers organized by use case (streaming, torrenting, gaming). It's also owned by Kape Technologies.

### Privacy

CyberGhost is based in Romania, which has decent privacy laws and rejected EU data retention directives. It publishes quarterly transparency reports and has passed independent audits. However, the Kape Technologies ownership casts the same shadow as it does on ExpressVPN. NordVPN's independent ownership and Panamanian base give it a clear edge for users who care about corporate transparency.

### Streaming and Torrenting

CyberGhost's streaming-optimized servers are labeled by platform (e.g., "Netflix US," "BBC iPlayer"), which makes server selection straightforward. This labeling is a nice UX touch for beginners. NordVPN handles this automatically via SmartPlay without requiring users to pick specific servers — arguably a more seamless experience.

For torrenting, CyberGhost offers dedicated P2P servers in select countries. NordVPN's P2P server network is larger, and its SOCKS5 proxy support adds flexibility that CyberGhost lacks.

### Pricing

CyberGhost's 2-year plan is aggressively priced, often around $2.19/month, making it one of the cheapest premium options. However, its 1-month plan is expensive (typically $12.99+), and its server performance can be inconsistent during peak hours. NordVPN's pricing is higher but includes more robust security features and a larger, more reliable server network.

**Verdict:** CyberGhost is a decent budget option for casual users. NordVPN wins on performance, privacy, and feature depth.
