A professional-looking business video used to require a camera, a presenter, a teleprompter, a lighting kit, and a video editor. Synthesia removes every one of those requirements. This walkthrough gets you from zero to an exported MP4 in under 10 minutes.

## What You Need Before You Start

- A Synthesia account (free trial available — 3 minutes of video on the free plan, enough for this exercise)
- A 150-word script (we'll give you one below)
- 10 minutes

No camera. No microphone. No video editing experience required.

## The 7 Steps

**Step 1 — Sign up for Synthesia**

Go to [Synthesia](https://synthesia.io/?via=jessie-pinedo) and create your free account. The free plan gives you enough video time to complete this exercise. Paid plans start at $29/month if you want to scale beyond the trial.

**Step 2 — Click "Create Video" and choose a template**

From the dashboard, click **+ New video**. You can start from a blank canvas or pick a template. For your first video, click **Blank** — it's faster to learn from a clean slate than to untangle someone else's template structure.

**Step 3 — Choose your avatar**

You'll see a grid of AI avatars. Scroll through the options — there are 125+ across different genders, ages, skin tones, and styles (formal business attire, casual, studio backdrop). Pick one that matches the tone of your content. For a business explainer, go formal. For a creator tutorial, go casual.

Click the avatar. It appears in the video preview on the left side of the editor.

**Step 4 — Paste your script**

In the text field on the right, paste your script. Here's a 150-word example you can adapt:

> *"If you're building a content business in 2026 and you're still filming yourself on camera, you're working ten times harder than you need to be. AI video tools have crossed a quality threshold where your audience can't tell the difference — and most of them don't care anyway. What they care about is whether the information is useful.*
>
> *In this video, I'm going to show you the exact workflow I use to produce professional videos for clients, YouTube channels, and LinkedIn content — all without a single camera session. By the end, you'll understand why some of the fastest-growing channels on the platform are completely faceless.*
>
> *Let's get into it. First, let's talk about the tool that makes this possible."*

Keep scripts under 250 words per scene for natural pacing.

**Step 5 — Select a voice**

Below the script field, you'll see a voice selector. Synthesia voices are tied to your avatar but you can swap them. Pick a voice that matches your avatar's appearance and the content tone. Preview it by clicking the play icon. Adjust speaking pace if needed (there's a slider — 90–100% is the natural sweet spot).

**Step 6 — Hit Generate**

Click **Generate video** in the top right. Synthesia processes your video in 2–5 minutes depending on length. You'll get an email when it's done, or you can watch the progress bar in the dashboard.

**Step 7 — Preview and export MP4**

Open your rendered video. Watch it end to end. If anything sounds unnatural — pacing issues, awkward pauses, mispronounced words — go back to the script and adjust (Synthesia uses phonetic pronunciation if a word is repeatedly mispronounced). When you're satisfied, click **Download** and export as MP4. Standard HD (1080p) is fine for most platforms.

## Common Pitfalls

**Script too long for free credits.** The free plan gives you 3 minutes of video time total across your account. A 250-word script at natural pace runs about 90 seconds — well within limits for a first project.

**Avatar mismatch with brand.** A fitness coach avatar in formal business wear looks wrong for a workout tutorial. Match the avatar's style to your content category before you're 500 scripts deep.

**Monotone script.** Synthesia reads what you write. If your script has no natural breaks, no emphasis, no contractions — it sounds flat. Write like you speak. Use sentence fragments. Use em dashes. Punch key words.

**Skipping the preview.** AI voices occasionally mispronounce industry terms or proper nouns. Always watch the full video before downloading.
