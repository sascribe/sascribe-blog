## What's New in 2026

NordVPN hasn't been sitting still. The 2026 iteration brings several meaningful updates that go beyond marketing fluff:

**Threat Protection Pro** has matured significantly. It now acts as a standalone security layer — blocking trackers, scanning downloads for malware, and filtering malicious URLs even when you're not connected to a VPN server. It's not a replacement for dedicated antivirus software, but it's a genuinely useful addition that adds real value to the subscription.

**Meshnet** continues to be one of NordVPN's most underrated features. It lets you route traffic through your own devices or trusted friends' devices, creating a private mesh network. This is incredibly useful for secure file sharing, LAN gaming across locations, or accessing your home network while traveling. No other major VPN offers anything quite like it at this level of polish.

**Double VPN and Obfuscated Servers** are still present and have been expanded to more locations. Double VPN routes your traffic through two servers instead of one, adding an extra encryption layer. Obfuscated servers disguise your VPN traffic to look like regular HTTPS traffic — critical for users in restrictive networks.

## Privacy and Security: The Foundation

Let's address the question I see constantly on Reddit: *Is NordVPN actually no-logs?*

Yes, and it's verified. NordVPN has undergone multiple independent audits by Deloitte confirming their no-logs policy. Their infrastructure runs on RAM-only servers, meaning data is physically incapable of persisting after a reboot. They're headquartered in Panama, which has no mandatory data retention laws and sits outside the Five Eyes, Nine Eyes, and Fourteen Eyes surveillance alliances.

The encryption stack is solid: AES-256 by default, with the NordLynx protocol (their WireGuard implementation) offering an excellent balance of speed and security. The kill switch — which cuts your internet connection if the VPN drops — works reliably across Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, and Android. I tested it deliberately by force-disconnecting the VPN mid-session, and it caught every drop during my testing period.

One thing I appreciate is transparency. NordVPN publishes a warrant canary and has been open about past incidents, including the 2019 server breach. Their response — migrating to diskless infrastructure and commissioning external audits — was the right move, and the current security posture reflects that lesson learned.

## Real-World Speed Performance

I'm not going to promise you specific numbers because VPN speeds depend heavily on your ISP, location, server load, and time of day. What I *can* tell you is how NordVPN performed relative to my baseline connection during testing.

**Nearby servers (same country):** I consistently saw 85-92% of my baseline download speeds using NordLynx. That's excellent. For a 500 Mbps connection, you're looking at very usable speeds for anything short of massive file transfers.

**Mid-range servers (neighboring countries):** Speeds dropped to roughly 70-80% of baseline. Still more than enough for 4K streaming.

**Distant servers (cross-continent):** This is where every VPN takes a hit. I saw 40-60% of baseline speeds on US-to-Asia and Europe-to-Australia routes. NordVPN handles this better than most, but physics is physics — data traveling thousands of miles through encrypted tunnels will always be slower.

**Peak hour congestion** is real. During evening hours in popular regions (US East Coast, UK), I noticed occasional slowdowns. NordVPN's 6,000+ server network in 111 countries helps mitigate this — you can usually switch to a less crowded server nearby — but it's worth noting.
