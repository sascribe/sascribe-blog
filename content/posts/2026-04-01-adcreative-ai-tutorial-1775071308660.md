If you've ever stared at a blank canvas in Facebook Ads Manager, struggling to come up with ad creatives that actually convert, you're not alone. Most marketers burn hours designing variations, testing headlines, and tweaking visuals — only to watch their click-through rates flatline. The reality is that creative fatigue kills campaigns faster than bad targeting ever will. That's exactly where AI-powered ad design tools come in, and in this tutorial, I'm going to walk you through how to create high-converting Facebook ads using [AdCreative AI](https://sascribe.com/go/adcreative) from start to finish. Whether you're running ads for an ecommerce store, a local business, or a SaaS product, this guide will save you time and dramatically improve your ad performance.

## What You'll Need Before Getting Started

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, let's make sure you have everything ready. There's nothing worse than getting halfway through a workflow and realizing you're missing a key asset.

**Prerequisites:**

- An active AdCreative AI account (a free trial works fine for following along)
- Access to your Facebook Business Manager and Ads Manager
- Your brand assets: logo file (PNG preferred), brand colors (hex codes), and any existing brand fonts
- A clear understanding of your target audience and campaign objective
- At least one product or service you want to advertise
- Landing page URL where you'll be sending traffic

If you don't have an account yet, you can sign up and explore the platform through [AdCreative AI](https://sascribe.com/go/adcreative) before proceeding.

Got everything? Good. Let's build some ads.

## Step 1: Set Up Your Brand Profile

This is the foundation of everything you'll create, so don't rush through it.

Once you log into your AdCreative AI dashboard, the first thing you'll want to do is create a brand profile. Head to the **Brands** section in the left sidebar and click **Add New Brand**.

Here's what to fill in:

- **Brand Name:** Enter your business or product name exactly as you want it to appear on ads.
- **Brand Logo:** Upload a high-resolution PNG with a transparent background. This ensures your logo looks clean on any ad background.
- **Brand Colors:** Input your primary and secondary brand colors using hex codes. The AI uses these to generate on-brand creatives automatically.
- **Brand Description:** Write a brief 2-3 sentence description of what your brand does and who it serves. The AI references this when generating copy.

**Pro Tip:** If you serve multiple product lines or audiences, create separate brand profiles for each. This keeps your creatives targeted rather than generic.

## Step 2: Connect Your Facebook Ad Account

Integration is where AdCreative AI really starts to shine. Navigate to **Integrations** in your settings and connect your Facebook Ads account. You'll need to authorize AdCreative AI through Facebook Business Manager.

Why does this matter? Two reasons:

1. **Direct publishing.** You can push creatives directly to your Facebook Ads Manager without downloading and re-uploading files.
2. **Performance scoring.** The platform pulls data from your connected ad accounts to refine its AI scoring model over time. The more data it has, the smarter its creative recommendations become.

The connection process takes about 60 seconds. Just follow the OAuth prompts, select the ad accounts you want to link, and confirm.

## Step 3: Generate Your Ad Creatives

Now for the part you actually came here for.

Go to **Generate Creatives** from the main dashboard. You'll be prompted to select a few parameters:

- **Ad Size:** For Facebook feed ads, select **1080x1080** (square) or **1200x628** (landscape). I recommend starting with 1080x1080 since it takes up more real estate in the mobile feed.
- **Target Audience:** Describe who you're targeting. Be specific — "female ecommerce entrepreneurs aged 28-45 in the US" gives the AI much better context than "business owners."
- **Ad Copy/Text:** You can either write your own headline and description or let the AI generate them. I suggest letting the AI take a first pass, then editing manually. The AI-generated headlines are surprisingly good at incorporating urgency and benefit-driven language.
- **Call to Action:** Select your CTA — Shop Now, Learn More, Sign Up, Get Offer, etc.
- **Product Image or Background:** Upload a product photo or let the AI select from stock visuals.

Click **Generate** and wait about 15-20 seconds. The platform will serve up multiple creative variations, each with a **conversion score** from 1 to 100.

**This scoring system is the killer feature.** It's trained on hundreds of millions of ad data points and predicts which creative is most likely to convert. In my experience, creatives scoring above 80 consistently outperform ones I design manually in Canva or Figma.

## Step 4: Review, Edit, and Select Your Top Performers

Don't just grab the first creative with a high score. Spend a few minutes reviewing the batch.

Here's my review process:

1. **Sort by conversion score** — Start with the highest-scored creatives.
2. **Check brand consistency** — Does the creative feel like your brand? Sometimes the AI takes creative liberties with layout. If a design uses your colors and logo well but the composition feels off, use the built-in editor to adjust.
3. **Read the copy out loud** — AI-generated ad copy is good but not perfect. Look for awkward phrasing, overpromises, or anything that doesn't sound like how your brand actually speaks. Edit directly inside the platform.
4. **Pick 3-5 variations** — You want enough creatives to run a meaningful A/B test in Facebook Ads Manager. Three is the minimum I'd recommend. Five is ideal.

**Pro Tip:** Pay attention to creatives where the AI has used different visual approaches — maybe one emphasizes the product, another uses a lifestyle image, and a third leans on bold typography. These visual differences will give you genuine insight into what your audience responds to.

## Step 5: Export or Push Directly to Facebook Ads Manager

You have two options here:

**Option A: Direct Push**
If you connected your Facebook account in Step 2, you can push your selected creatives directly to an existing or new campaign. Click the **Push to Ads** button, select the campaign and ad set, and the creatives populate automatically. This is the fastest workflow I've seen in any ad tool.

**Option B: Download and Upload Manually**
If you prefer more control, download the creatives as PNG or JPEG files and upload them to Facebook Ads Manager yourself. This is useful if you want to make final tweaks in another design tool first.

Regardless of which path you choose, make sure your Facebook campaign is set up with proper audience targeting, budget, and placement settings before you go live.

## Step 6: Monitor Performance and Iterate

Launching the ads is only half the battle. Here's where most people drop the ball.

After your ads have been running for 3-5 days (and have accumulated at least 1,000 impressions per creative), come back to AdCreative AI and check the **Creative Insights** section. The platform compares its predicted conversion scores against your actual ad performance data.

Use this feedback loop to:

- **Kill underperformers early.** If a creative scored 85 but has a CTR below 1% after sufficient impressions, pause it.
- **Double down on winners.** Increase budget allocation to creatives that are outperforming.
- **Generate new variations.** Use the winning creative as a baseline and generate fresh iterations. AdCreative AI lets you create variations of existing designs, which is perfect for combating creative fatigue without starting from scratch.

I typically refresh my Facebook ad creatives every 10-14 days. With this tool, that process takes me about 20 minutes instead of half a day.

## Advanced Pro Tips for Better Results

After using this platform across dozens of campaigns, here are a few lessons that aren't obvious on day one:

- **Use the Text Generator AI separately.** Before generating creatives, use the dedicated text generation feature to create 10-15 headline variations. Pick the best 3 and feed them into the creative generator manually. This gives you more control over messaging.
- **Test square vs. landscape aggressively.** I've seen cases where a 1080x1080 creative outperforms the same design in 1200x628 by 40%. The AI scores each format independently, so generate both.
- **Leverage Creative Scoring for client buy-in.** If you're an agency, showing a client that their ad creative has a predicted conversion score of 92 out of 100 builds massive confidence. It's data-driven reassurance.
- **Pair with retargeting campaigns.** Generate separate creative batches for cold and warm audiences. The messaging should be fundamentally different — cold audiences need education, warm audiences need a reason to act now.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need design experience to use AdCreative AI for Facebook ads?

No. The entire platform is built so that non-designers can generate professional, high-converting ad creatives. You upload your brand assets, set your parameters, and the AI handles the design work.

### How accurate is the conversion scoring system?

It's trained on a massive dataset and improves over time with your own performance data. It's not a guarantee, but in my testing, creatives scoring 80+ consistently outperform lower-scored alternatives. Treat it as a strong signal, not a certainty.

### Can I use AdCreative AI for other platforms besides Facebook?

Absolutely. The tool supports formats for Instagram, Google Display, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Pinterest, and more. The workflow is nearly identical — you just select a different ad size during the generation step.

### How many creatives can I generate per month?

This depends on your plan. The starter tiers give you a set number of monthly downloads, while higher-tier plans offer unlimited or near-unlimited generation. Check the current pricing on [AdCreative AI](https://sascribe.com/go/adcreative) for the latest details.
